# Character Liveliness Bias

## Purpose

Every character should be a little more interesting, cute, lively, or actable
than their plain setting would imply. This does not mean making everyone bubbly.
It means every role needs a small surplus of human texture: a funny dodge, a
petty habit, a strange nickname, a soft contradiction, a badly timed joke, or a
piece of body language the player can look forward to seeing again.

## Default Rule

When designing or drafting any character, apply `liveliness +1` after the
serious role is defined.

- A cold character becomes dry, specific, and faintly funny under pressure.
- A quiet character has a tiny ritual, misfire, or unfinished phrase.
- A mature character has a domestic quirk, practical tease, or private softness.
- A tragic character still has ordinary appetites, bad jokes, impatience, and
  small vanity.
- A villain or antagonist still gets charm, wit, politeness, taste, or a
  disarming social habit.

## Required Character Hooks

Each major character needs at least two of these:

- playful nickname for a person, object, system, place, or rule
- repeated tiny physical bit: counting twice, tapping a cup, hiding a sleeve
- sentence-end texture: `嘛`, `啦`, `欸`, `吧`, `喔`, a question flick, a cut-off
- domestic preference: snack, drink, superstition, tool, smell, song, weather
- social misfire: joke lands wrong, politeness arrives too late, pride leaks
- object affection: treats a machine, document, ticket, or garment like a person
- cute contradiction: fierce but fussy, cold but precise-helpful, lazy but
  observant, polite but sharp

## Punctuation Bias

Punctuation must keep the character actable:

- Do not let a dialogue block become all full stops.
- Add pressure-specific marks before the line is finalized, not after.
- Use softer marks for liveliness where voice supports them: `？`, `？！`, `……`,
  `——`, `嘛`, `啦`, `欸`, `吧`, `喔`, `呀`.
- Preserve serious moments by contrast: a lively character going plain should
  feel like a mask has dropped.

## Output Contract

Before drafting, character cards or visible briefs must expose:

- `charm_hook`: what makes this character fun to watch in ordinary moments
- `liveliness_floor`: the minimum amount of playfulness or actable texture
- `punctuation_play`: which marks and particles belong to this character
- at least one memory point that is cute, odd, petty, or lively

## Failure Signs

- The character is accurate to the setting but dull to click through.
- A serious role removes all humor, looseness, or social friction.
- Dialogue is correct but every line lands like a report.
- Punctuation is technically varied but not character-owned.
- The user cannot name one repeatable fun thing about the character.
