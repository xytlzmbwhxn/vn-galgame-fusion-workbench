# Interiority Is Not Narration

## Call This When

Before writing or revising thought rows, first-person passages, POV narration, or psychological texture in a visual novel scene.

## Core Rule

Thought is not any sentence placed inside parentheses. If a camera can film it or a microphone can hear it, the row is narration, stage, dialogue, or sound, not inner monologue.

Inner text should carry private pressure that cannot be fully shown by sprite, action, or spoken line.

## Thought Row Jobs

A valid thought row should do at least one job:

- reveal an unsaid motive
- read a clue in a character-specific way
- expose a contradiction between role and desire
- make the next choice harder
- admit fear, jealousy, shame, tenderness, or calculation the speaker hides
- compare two possible actions before one is chosen
- catch a bodily reaction only when it changes the next move
- misinterpret something in a way that belongs to this POV

## Not Thought

These are not inner monologue by themselves:

- visible action
- object detail
- light, sound, weather, room layout
- production direction
- lyrical metaphor with no decision pressure
- a summary of the scene's theme
- a translation of what the dialogue already implies

Bad-row diagnosis:

- `撤销键暗了下去。桌上剩一小片热敏纸边...` = screen/object narration.
- `取件单上的字很小...她把它折进文件夹...` = visible action and object detail.

Either row can be useful as narration or stage if it has a click function, but neither should count as psychology.

## Placement Rules

Use thought:

- before a choice
- after a line lands and the protagonist cannot answer honestly
- at a conversation pause
- when a clue changes the protagonist's next action
- when a social mask cracks internally but not visibly

Avoid thought:

- in the middle of fast call-and-response unless the interruption is itself the point
- when a sprite expression or SFX can carry the same beat
- when the line only makes prose prettier
- when it explains the moral lesson

## Display Rules

Readable previews may use `（内心文本）`, but the parentheses must mean "private interiority," not "any narration I want to soften."

Thought can be less grammatically complete than narration. It may use withheld nouns, self-correction, short private questions, or unfinished admission, as long as the voice belongs to the POV.

## Failure Signs

- Thought rows are mostly camera-visible.
- The protagonist's mind never chooses, hides, misreads, or pays a cost.
- The thought repeats the previous dialogue.
- The inner line sounds like the author explaining craft or theme.
- A decorative metaphor replaces an actual psychological decision.
