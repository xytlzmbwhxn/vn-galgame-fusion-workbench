# Line Energy And Punctuation

## Call This When

Before drafting or revising dialogue, especially when lines sound flat, over-controlled, or interchangeable.

## Goal

Use punctuation as acting direction, not decoration. A visual novel line should tell the actor and the player where the speaker bites, dodges, rushes, swallows, or loses control.

## Inputs

- speaker pressure state
- speaker social mask
- forbidden topic
- object in hand
- relationship leverage
- current scene tempo

## Procedure

1. Pick a line energy before writing the sentence: jab, corner, stall, cover, plead, mock, slip, recover, or cut off.
2. Match punctuation to the energy:
   - full stop: control, procedure, refusal, exhausted certainty
   - question mark: pressure, challenge, forced intimacy, disbelief
   - exclamation: loss of mask, sudden command, panic, comic snap
   - ellipsis: swallowed noun, delayed confession, fear of being heard
   - dash or abrupt cut: interruption, self-correction, almost-said truth
   - comma chain: bargaining, nervous speed, avoiding the main word
   - filler plus punctuation: relaxation after pressure, e.g. `嗯……当然？`,
     `好吧。`, `诶，等等。`
3. Give every major character a punctuation budget. A careful character should not suddenly spray exclamation marks; an evasive character should not finish every line cleanly.
4. Replace neutral replies with charged replies. The line must answer, dodge, wound, flirt, bargain, or test.
5. Read a dialogue block without speaker names. If punctuation and sentence shape do not reveal the speaker, rewrite the block.
6. Use one visual or sound interruption after a punctuation turn so the textbox rhythm supports the acting.
7. Apply a liveliness pass after the functional pass. Even a cold, tired, cruel, or serious character needs one character-owned mark of play: a dry question, a nickname, a swallowed ending, a small `嘛/欸/吧`, a badly timed joke, or a physical bit that gives the line bounce.
8. Do not confuse energy with constant sharpness. Cute/casual scenes need release
   beats: particles, softened endings, half-jokes, or procedural words used as
   emotional cover. If every line only corners or judges, the scene becomes stiff.

## Output Fields

- line_energy
- punctuation_intent
- speaker_budget
- interruption_or_recovery
- name-hidden readability

## Failure Signs

- every line ends with the same full stop
- questions sound like forms, not pressure
- exclamation marks appear without a broken mask
- ellipses replace real subtext
- a character's punctuation contradicts their voice card
- dialogue is clean but cannot be acted
- punctuation is varied statistically but still feels hard, flat, or unowned by the character
- all repaired thoughts become questions, with no filler, particle, retreat, or
  relaxed recovery beat
