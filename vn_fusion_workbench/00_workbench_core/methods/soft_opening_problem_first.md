# Soft Opening: Problem First, Identity Later

## Call This When

Load before drafting or revising the opening of a VN/Galgame scene, especially when the draft starts with direct ID exposition such as narrator name, age, role, then job list.

## Source Calibration

This method abstracts opening patterns from well-known VN structures and public
descriptions, without quoting or copying source prose:

- school-life route openings: establish dissatisfaction, place and encounter pressure before a bare ID card.
- science/urban mystery openings: use an abnormal event and an interpretive frame before the broader premise explains itself.
- ritual/conflict openings: make identity matter through action and obligation.
- gothic/guide openings: create an immediate place-question before full self-knowledge.
- slice-of-life mystery openings: let ordinary group texture be undercut by a small wrongness.

## Rule

Open with a question-bearing object, place, sound, contradiction, or small rule failure. Let the protagonist's identity appear through what they notice, touch, avoid, count, misread, or get called by.

## Blocking Anti-Pattern

Avoid opening with a self-ID block: narrator name, age, role, then job list. This is allowed only for intentionally bureaucratic parody, in-world dossiers, character profile pages, or comedic cold opens where the stiffness is immediately broken by scene action.

## Better Opening Shapes

### 1. Object With A Problem

Start from an object that should be ordinary but has become suspicious: a note in a fridge door, a wet umbrella with no owner, a receipt with the wrong name, or a club ledger missing one line. Identity emerges from how the POV handles it.

### 2. Place With A Rule

Show a location already governed by a small rule. Then let someone violate or misread the rule. The reader learns role, relation, and tone by watching who enforces the rule and who teases it.

### 3. Sound Before Explanation

Use a sound or interruption to pull the POV out of routine: bell, fridge hum, door scrape, phone vibration, broadcast mistake. The first click should create a question, not answer a resume.

### 4. Name Through Address

Let another character call the POV by name, nickname, role, or teasing title. This is softer than self-introduction because it also reveals relationship.

### 5. Psychology As A Leak

Before formal exposition, use a private reaction: the POV hides a note instead of reading it, notices a detail they should not care about, edits a rule to sound less jealous, or counts objects twice.

## Opening Checklist

- First 3 clicks contain a question, object, or pressure.
- First 10 clicks establish place through usable objects.
- The protagonist is identifiable by behavior before self-labeling.
- Another character introduces name/relationship through address or action.
- Exposition is delayed until the reader wants it.
- The opening's first rule or object returns later as a callback.
- No direct self-ID block unless intentionally parodied and broken.

## Repair Move For Direct ID Openings

Convert a direct bio opening into: object with problem -> POV action -> private hesitation -> another character's address/name -> only then a compact role inference.

Example direction: begin with the pink sticky note caught in the club fridge door; show the POV writing 违规物 in the ledger; let Xia Li enter and call him 顾会计.

## Output Contract

For revised openings, note: opening hook object, opening question, delayed identity reveal mechanism, first relationship signal, and callback object/rule.
