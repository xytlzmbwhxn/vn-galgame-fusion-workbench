# Chinese VN Style Guardrails

Load this file before drafting Chinese visual novel prose or dialogue. The goal is to prevent generic assistant prose from entering the script.

## Root Causes Of AI-Flavored Fiction

- Balanced explanation replaces lived pressure.
- Abstract nouns replace objects and behavior.
- Characters state their emotions too cleanly.
- Every paragraph tries to sound wise.
- Contrasts arrive as neat paired clauses.
- Scene endings summarize theme.
- Dialogue becomes therapy, debate, or exposition.
- The narrator overuses mist, light, silence, fate, memory, and "as if" imagery.
- Everyone speaks with the same polite rhythm.

## Forbidden Creative Moves

During creative drafting, avoid these patterns unless quoting a character who is intentionally pompous:

- `不是 X，而是 Y`
- `并非 X，而是 Y`
- `与其说 X，不如说 Y`
- `真正的 X 是 Y`
- `这不只是 X，更是 Y`
- `某种意义上`
- `他/她意识到`
- `他/她突然明白`
- `空气仿佛凝固`
- `命运的齿轮`
- `说不清是 X 还是 Y`
- `复杂的情绪`
- `无法言说`
- `像有一只无形的手`
- `这是关于...的故事`
- `也许，这就是...`

Do not replace one banned formula with a synonym pair. Remove the explanatory impulse.

## Replacement Tactics

Use action:

- Let a character fold a receipt until the ink breaks.
- Let someone answer the wrong question.
- Let a sprite smile while the hand on the table tightens.
- Let a phone notification arrive too late.
- Let the player choose whether to look at the stain, the door, or the person lying.

Use concrete pressure:

- Deadlines, witnesses, debts, hunger, fever, curfew, broken tools, rain in shoes, an unread message, a locked cabinet.

Use omission:

- Characters dodge names.
- A sentence stops before the dangerous noun.
- Someone quotes an old rule instead of answering.
- A choice menu withholds the "honest" option because the protagonist cannot say it yet.

Use asymmetry:

- Let one side of a conflict be funny, petty, tired, or practical.
- Let emotional turns arrive through bad timing.
- Let characters misunderstand each other in ways that reveal history.

## Dialogue Voice Rules

Every major character needs:

- A default sentence length.
- A pressure sentence length.
- A favorite dodge.
- A taboo word.
- A lie style.
- A request style.
- A cruelty style.
- A tenderness style.

Dialogue should not sound like polished essays. Use interruption, repetition, unfinished clauses, banal details, jokes that miss, and tactical silence.

Useful line functions:

- Attack.
- Deflect.
- Bargain.
- Test.
- Flatter.
- Stall.
- Confess sideways.
- Change subject.
- Offer a false memory.
- Ask for something under the cover of asking something else.

## Narration Rules

Prefer:

- Specific nouns over mood labels.
- Verbs over adjective clusters.
- One sharp image over stacked metaphors.
- Body behavior over named feelings.
- Scene-specific detail over stock atmosphere.

Avoid:

- Explaining the theme after the scene already shows it.
- Saying a character feels fear, guilt, sadness, tenderness, or confusion without behavior.
- Describing silence as heavy, long, suffocating, or frozen.
- Ending scenes with a neat philosophical sentence.
- Repeating "仿佛", "似乎", "像是" as a way to soften every image.

## VN Text Box Rhythm

- Keep most ADV boxes to one or two short sentences.
- Put a click break where the player's expectation changes.
- Use a solo word or short fragment only when the image/sound supports it.
- Avoid multi-clause narration in the middle of urgent dialogue.
- Let choices appear after a pressure turn, not after a neutral information dump.
- If the player must read lore, let them choose the order of inquiry.

## Choice Menu Copy

Choice labels can be:

- Spoken lines.
- Physical actions.
- Interior impulses.
- Questions.
- Lies.
- Silence.
- Evidence presentation.
- Resource spends.

Good menu labels carry attitude:

- "把录音笔推过去。"
- "问她为什么少了一把伞。"
- "装作没看见血。"
- "把最后一枚硬币留给明天。"

Weak labels over-explain category:

- "选择善良的做法。"
- "撒谎路线。"
- "询问更多信息。"
- "进入坏结局。"

## Revision Pass

Run these passes in order:

1. Formula pass: search for banned patterns and remove the sentence shape, not only the phrase.
2. Abstraction pass: replace abstract emotional nouns with behavior, props, or a choice.
3. Voice pass: hide name boxes and check whether speakers remain identifiable.
4. Textbox pass: split or cut lines that would tire the click rhythm.
5. Consequence pass: ensure choices produce visible feedback or are clearly expressive.
6. Silence pass: remove stock silence descriptions; use an action, sound, sprite, or menu delay.
7. Final line pass: cut any scene-ending sentence that explains what the player should feel.

## Quick Lint Terms

When editing Chinese prose, search for:

`不是|并非|而是|与其说|不如说|真正|某种意义|意识到|突然明白|仿佛|像是|似乎|空气|沉默|命运|齿轮|复杂|无法言说|一瞬间|心底|深处|救赎|羁绊`

Presence is not automatic failure. Repeated presence signals a style drift.
