# Style Rules

- Use Chinese Galgame readable rhythm: dialogue blocks, visible actions, short thought rows, and production cues.
- Dialogue preview must render as `【角色】『台词』`; inner thought must render as `【角色】（心理）`.
- Do not use thesis-like summaries, balanced contrast formulas, or abstract emotion reporting.
- Keep the copy-shop environment active: printer heat, toner smell, paper cutter, stapler, binding glue, page order, USB names, file timestamps.
- Tang Li may sound cute and casual, but her jokes should protect a real request.
- Xu Cheng is procedural and dry; warmth leaks through exact operations rather than speeches.
