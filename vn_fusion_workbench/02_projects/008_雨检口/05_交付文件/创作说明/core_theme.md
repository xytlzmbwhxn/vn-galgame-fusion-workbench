# Core Theme

## Central Question

When a system only protects what it can record, what does a person owe to someone who cannot safely be named?

## Dramatic Argument

The station can log a ticket, a door, a camera frame, a lost umbrella. It cannot log a half-spoken name, a delayed alarm, or the reason someone is afraid to go home.

The protagonist's pressure is not "break rules or follow rules." That is too clean. The real pressure in play is more practical:

- records can preserve evidence
- records can expose the person who needs cover
- mercy can save a person tonight
- mercy can destroy proof tomorrow

Good choices must wound something.

## Route-Level Theme Tests

- Register route: paper remembers what video can be made to forget.
- Monitor route: official proof can be used as a weapon by whoever controls the frame.
- Bad ends: the player protects one category too perfectly and loses the person, the proof, or both.

## Scene-Level Requirement

Each key scene must make the player choose between at least two valid loyalties:

- loyalty to procedure
- loyalty to a frightened person
- loyalty to future proof
- loyalty to a promise already made

No scene may end by explaining the theme. The last beat should leave an object, absence, or changed option.
